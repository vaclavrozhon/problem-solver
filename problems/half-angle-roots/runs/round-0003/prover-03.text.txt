Core ideas retained
- At v=e^{\pi i/(2n)} (θ=\pi/(2n)) one has [m]_v = \sin(mθ)/\sin θ.
- The summand can be written exactly as
  C_{n,i}:=\qbinom{n+i}{2i}_v = \frac{\prod_{k=n-i+1}^{n+i} \sin(kθ)}{\prod_{k=1}^{2i} \sin(kθ)}.
  An equivalent cosine form for 0≤i≤n−1 is C_{n,i} = \cos(iθ)\prod_{k=1}^{i-1}\cos^2(kθ)/\prod_{k=1}^{2i}\sin(kθ) (use sin((n±p)θ)=cos(pθ)). Note: C_{n,n}=1 (exact cancellation of zeros in the sine product); the cosine form has a 0/0 and should not be used at i=n.
- For i≈αn, log C_{n,i} = (2n/\pi) F(x) + O(\log n) with x=(\pi/2)α and F(x)=Λ(2x)+2Λ(\tfrac{\pi}{2}−x), where Λ(y)=−\int_0^y \log(2\sin t) dt is the Lobachevsky function.
- F has a unique maximizer at x⋆=\pi/6 (α⋆=1/3), with F(\pi/6)=3Λ(\pi/3)=Cl_2(\pi/3)=:v_3, the regular ideal tetrahedron volume; V(4_1)=2v_3. Hence max_i log C_{n,i}=(2n/\pi)v_3+O(\log n)=(V/\pi)n+O(\log n). By positivity, max C_{n,i} ≤ f_n ≤ (n+1) max C_{n,i}, so log f_n(e^{\pi i/n})=(V/\pi)n+O(\log n).

Rigorous lemmas to add (statements and proof sketches)
- Lemma A (Uniform sum–integral for log(2 sin)). Let θ=\pi/(2n). For 1≤M≤2n−1,
  ∑_{k=1}^M \log(2\sin(kθ)) = θ^{−1}\int_0^{Mθ} \log(2\sin t) dt + O(\log n),
  uniformly in M. Sketch: Split at K=\lfloor n^{2/3}\rfloor. For k≤K, \log(2\sin(kθ))=\log(2kθ)+O((kθ)^2), sum gives \log(K!) + K\log(2θ) + O(1)=θ^{−1}\int_0^{Kθ}\log(2\sin t)dt + O(\log n). For k>K, f(t)=\log(2\sin t) is C^1 and bounded away from its endpoint singularities on [Kθ, Mθ] (or has integrable behavior near Mθ≤\pi), so Euler–Maclaurin or monotone sum–integral bounds give O(1) error; the overall error is O(\log n). This handles both t≈0 and t≈\pi.
- Lemma B (Uniform sum–integral for log(2 cos)). For 1≤M≤n, with θ=\pi/(2n),
  ∑_{k=1}^M \log(2\cos(kθ)) = θ^{−1}\int_0^{Mθ} \log(2\cos t) dt + O(\log n),
  uniformly in M. Sketch: Same splitting; near t≈\pi/2, \log(2\cos t) behaves like \log(\pi/2−t), producing at most an O(\log n) contribution; away from the singularity, EM yields O(1).
- Lemma C (Variational formula). For i=\lfloor xn\rfloor, x∈[0,1],
  log C_{n,i} = \frac{2n}{\pi}\Big(2\int_0^{\frac{\pi x}{2}} \log\cos t\,dt − \int_0^{\pi x} \log\sin t\,dt\Big) + O(\log n),
  uniformly in x. Proof: Rewrite the numerator as cos sums using sin((n±p)θ)=cos(pθ), keep the denominator as a sin sum, and apply Lemmas A–B.
- Lemma D (Maximizer and concavity). Define G(x)=2∫_0^{\frac{\pi x}{2}} \log\cos t\,dt − ∫_0^{\pi x} \log\sin t\,dt. Then G′(x)=\pi\log(\cos(\tfrac{\pi x}{2})/\sin(\pi x)); G″(x)=−\tfrac{\pi^2}{2}\tan(\tfrac{\pi x}{2})−\pi^2\cot(\pi x)<0 on (0,1). Thus a unique maximizer at x⋆=1/3. Moreover, G(x) ≤ G(1/3) − c(x−1/3)^2 on [0,1] for some c>0.
- Lemma E (Value at maximizer). G(1/3)=3Λ(\pi/3)=Cl_2(\pi/3)=:v_3. Proof: Use F(x)=Λ(2x)+2Λ(\tfrac{\pi}{2}−x), F(\pi/6)=Λ(\pi/3)+2Λ(\pi/3)=3Λ(\pi/3), and Λ(y)=\tfrac12 Cl_2(2y). Cite Lewin (Polylogarithms) for 2 Cl_2(\pi/3)=3 Cl_2(2\pi/3) and v_3=Cl_2(\pi/3). V(4_1)=2v_3.
- Proposition (Max term and sum). There exists C>0 such that for all i, log C_{n,i} ≤ (V/\pi)n + C\log n, with equality up to O(\log n) for i=\lfloor n/3\rfloor. Consequently, (V/\pi)n − C\log n ≤ \log f_n(e^{\pi i/n}) ≤ (V/\pi)n + (C+1)\log n.
