# Problem — Asymptotics at half-angle roots and the figure-eight volume

For $n\in\mathbb{N}$, define the **symmetrized $q$-binomial** using a parameter $v$ by

$$
[m]_v \;=\; \frac{v^{m}-v^{-m}}{v-v^{-1}},\qquad
\qbinom{a}{b}_v \;=\; \frac{[a]_v [a-1]_v \cdots [a-b+1]_v}{[b]_v [b-1]_v \cdots [1]_v},
$$

with the convention that $\qbinom{a}{0}_v=1$ and $\qbinom{a}{b}_v=0$ if $b<0$ or $b>a$.
Set $q=v^2$. For each $n$, define

$$
f_n(q)\;=\;\sum_{i=0}^{n}\qbinom{n+i}{2i}_v .
$$

Let $V$ denote the hyperbolic volume of the figure-eight knot $4_1$ $(V\approx 2.0298832128)$.

**Claim.** With the specialization $q=e^{\pi i/n}$ (equivalently $v=e^{\pi i/(2n)}$), the sequence satisfies the asymptotic

$$
f_n\!\big(e^{\pi i/n}\big)\;=\;\exp\!\Big(\frac{V}{2\pi}\,n + o(n)\Big)\qquad\text{as }n\to\infty.
$$

In words: $f_n(e^{\pi i/n})$ grows like $\exp\!\big(\tfrac{V}{2\pi}n\big)$ as $n\to\infty$.
