Specific gaps, errors, and corrective lemmas
1) On “Int(cl(S)) is saturated.”
- The given sketch is invalid: re‑rooting does not preserve membership in a cylinder, and open sets are unions of cylinders. Hence, from (G,ρ) ∈ Int(cl(S)) it does not follow that (G,u) ∈ Int(cl(S)). You do not need this statement for the ND result. Remove it; keep only “cl(S) is saturated,” which is true and used below.
- What you can safely use: closure of a saturated set is saturated.
  Proof. If S is saturated and (G,ρ) ∈ cl(S), then for any vertex u and radius t there exists r≫dist(ρ,u)+t and (H_r,σ_r) ∈ S with B_r(H_r,σ_r) ≅ B_r(G,ρ). Let u_r be the image of u; saturation gives (H_r,u_r) ∈ S and B_t(H_r,u_r) ≅ B_t(G,u), so (G,u) ∈ cl(S).

2) Cylinder characterization of nowhere denseness (use this everywhere).
- Cylinders C(F):=G_{•}^{F} are clopen in the rooted ultrametric space. A set S is nowhere dense iff for every rooted r‑ball F there exists an r′‑ball F′ with C(F′) ⊆ C(F) \ S. Your Current Output states precisely this criterion for S_P, formulated in root‑free terms; good.

3) Saturations of cylinders are not open (fix quotient remarks and provide a counterexample).
- Let D≥2 and let F be the 1‑ball consisting of a leaf root. C(F) = rooted graphs where the root has degree 1. Its saturation Sat(C(F)) = {(G,ρ): G has a leaf somewhere} is not open: pick (H,σ) where the unique leaf of H lies at distance ≫ r from σ. Any neighborhood C(B_r(H,σ)) contains graphs K in which the far leaf is eliminated (modify only outside the r‑ball), so K ∉ Sat(C(F)). Therefore Sat(C(F)) is not open. Consequently, π(C(F)) need not be open in any unrooted quotient constructed from R via forgetting the root.
- Action: keep quotient language only if you define U_q with the final (quotient) topology and refrain from claiming that images of cylinders are open. For the ND result, you do not need π to be open.

4) Parity/scope corrections for bridges in D‑regular graphs.

- Proposed route (to be completed):

  2) Build degree‑preserving marker gadgets ensuring that for comeagre many rooted graphs, a unique marked neighbor (and iteratively, a unique marked path) exists; this yields a comeagre set Good on which finitely many T_{p} cover all finite re‑rootings.
