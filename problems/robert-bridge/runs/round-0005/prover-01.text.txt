Specific flaws and corrections
1) Off-by-one in the metric equivalence.
- You state “d_•((G,ρ),(H,σ)) < 2^{-r} iff B_r(G,ρ)≅B_r(H,σ)”. Correct is: d_• ≤ 2^{-r} iff B_r agrees; and d_• < 2^{-r} iff B_{r+1} agrees. Please fix all uses to avoid later mismatches in radii.

2) “Int(cl(S)) is saturated” is unproven and unnecessary.
- Your sketch “open sets are unions of cylinders and re-rooting preserves membership” is invalid: re-rooting does not preserve membership in a given cylinder. Do not use this claim. What you need and can prove rigorously is: closure of a saturated set is saturated.
- Lemma (closure of saturated is saturated). If S⊆G_{\bullet,D} is saturated and (G,ρ)∈cl(S), then for any u∈V(G) and t∈N there exists r≫dist(ρ,u)+t and (H_r,σ_r)∈S with B_r(H_r,σ_r)≅B_r(G,ρ); let u_r be the image of u; then (H_r,u_r)∈S (by saturation) and B_t(H_r,u_r)≅B_t(G,u), hence (G,u)∈cl(S).
- Consequence: The cylinder refinement criterion yields a root-free ND characterization for S_P (already in your Current Output). No further saturation of Int(cl(S)) is needed.

3) Saturations of cylinders are not open; quotient arguments must be handled with care.
- Counterexample: Let F be the 1-ball of a leaf-root. C(F)={root has degree 1}. Its saturation Sat(C(F))={has a leaf somewhere} is not open: in any neighborhood of a rooted graph whose unique leaf lies very far, you can delete that far leaf outside the prescribed ball without altering the ball; hence the neighborhood intersects the complement. Thus, for the forget-the-root map π, π(C(F)) need not be open in any unrooted space derived from G_{\bullet,D} via quotient. Avoid relying on π being open.

5) Theorem 14 (bridgeless is nowhere dense): Δ≤D vs Δ=D requires explicit, degree-aware gadgets.

Concretely: saturate all boundary degrees except two designated vertices x,y by internal wiring; leave x,y with exactly one free stub each. Then any D-regular completion must use exactly one external edge at x and exactly one at y; force that x and y lie in different infinite components beyond the ball and are connected by a single edge (or construct two disjoint “ports” that any completion must connect uniquely). Provide the finite gadget and a completion lemma guaranteeing nonemptiness in Δ=D.

Ambiguity sets Amb_k: fix the flaw and outline a correct rigidification
- Flaw in current sketch: Decorating only beyond a single boundary vertex b cannot break (G,u)≅(G,v) if u and v are symmetric within the fixed r-ball; outside decorations seen via b are the same from both roots and can be permuted by an isomorphism extending an interior symmetry. Thus the proposed “hub at b with beacons” is insufficient.
- Corrected strategy (outer-shell rigidification, Δ≤D, D≥3): From a nonempty cylinder C(F) and k, choose R≫r+k and define a refined r′-ball F′ with layers from r+1 to R under your control. For each boundary vertex at level r, attach exactly one new neighbor at level r+1 and, beyond it, build a large finite “signature tree” (degree ≤D) whose leaf signatures are pairwise non-isomorphic across different boundary vertices. This forces any automorphism fixing the outermost sphere to fix all vertices at level r+1, hence at level r, and inductively all vertices within distance ≤k of the root. Additionally, vary the depths of certain unique beacons so that, for each vertex w at distance ≤k and its distinct neighbors u≠v, the multisets of distances from u and v to the nearest beacon(s) differ; this guarantees (G,u) and (G,v) are not isomorphic as rooted graphs in any completion. Only vertices outside B_r receive new edges.
- Δ= D: first choose R≥r so that some boundary vertex has spare degree (nonempty cylinder implies existence). Saturate degrees within the new shells so all vertices at distance <R have degree D in F′ while leaving exactly one “port” at some boundary vertex to anchor the rigidifying trees. Use the same beacon variation and provide a completion lemma showing D-regular completions exist.
- Deliverables to make this rigorous: (i) a clear definition of the signature trees to ensure pairwise non-isomorphism; (ii) precise degree bookkeeping; (iii) proof that the pointwise stabilizer of the outer sphere is trivial on the inner radius ≤k; (iv) for Δ=D, a completion lemma ensuring nonemptiness of the refined cylinder.
- Outcome: Each Amb_k is nowhere dense. With a standard re-rooting template coverage (saturation by countably many finite path moves), one can then deduce: the saturation of a nowhere-dense set is meagre on a comeagre domain, completing the meagre half for D≥3 (details still to be written).
