Examples
- Failure (no PNE): the directed 3-cycle with all edges negative. It is neither undirected, nor structurally balanced, nor acyclic; indeed no PNE exists.
- DAG: any orientation of a tree; the greedy reverse-topological assignment constructs a PNE.
- SCC mix: a sink SCC that is undirected plus a predecessor SCC that is structurally balanced; the SCC theorem applies.

Obstacles
- Structural balance is necessary for directed signed SCCs in the sense that a strongly connected component with a signed cycle of negative sign that cannot be switched away can destroy PNE existence (e.g., matching pennies-like motifs).

Next steps
- Tighten SCC conditions: identify minimal SCC classes (beyond undirected/structurally balanced) that guarantee a PNE for any external field, e.g., exact potential on C up to additive separable terms, or classes with acyclic best-response graphs.
- Algorithmics: linear-time PNE construction for DAGs; polynomial-time via SCC decomposition with per-SCC solvers (potential maximization or lattice-theoretic fixed points).
